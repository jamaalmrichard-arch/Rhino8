### 2.5 DLAB Certification Significance

Your DLAB certification is notable:
- The DLAB tests aptitude for learning foreign languages. A passing score (95+ out of 164) qualifies you for linguist/intelligence MOS training at the Defense Language Institute (DLI).
- Scores of 100+ open Category II languages (French, German, Spanish). Scores of 110+ open Category III (Russian, Korean, etc.). Scores of 120+ open Category IV (Arabic, Chinese, Japanese).
- While the DLAB itself is not a medal, it represents a significant cognitive achievement and appears on your service record. It demonstrates aptitude for intelligence and linguistics work -- relevant for your resume and any security clearance applications.
- If you attended DLI or any language training, those courses carry substantial ACE college credit recommendations.

## 3. How to Get Your Official Records

You need three sets of records because you served in three different components.

### 3.1 DD-214 (Marine Corps Active Duty)

Your DD-214 is the single most important military document. It lists all awards, dates of service, character of discharge, and MOS.

**How to Request:**

| Method | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Online (Fastest)** | Go to [eVetRecs](https://www.archives.gov/veterans/military-service-records) at archives.gov. Create account via ID.me verification. Submit request digitally. |
| **By Mail** | Complete [SF-180 form](https://www.archives.gov/veterans/military-service-records/standard-form-180.html) and mail to: **National Personnel Records Center (NPRC), 1 Archives Drive, St. Louis, MO 63138** |
| **By Fax** | Fax completed SF-180 to: 314-801-9195 |
| **By Phone** | Call NPRC at 314-801-0800 (best times: 7-10 AM CT or after 3 PM CT to avoid hold times) |
| **Cost** | FREE for veterans requesting their own records |
| **Timeline** | 2-8 weeks typical; can take longer |

**Important:** You should have multiple DD-214s -- one for each period of active duty. Your reserve contract (Oct 2000 - Apr 2001) may have a DD-214 or equivalent separation document. Your active duty period (Mar 2003 - Dec 2003) should definitely have a DD-214.

### 3.2 Awards Verification (Marine Corps HQ)

If your DD-214 is missing awards you believe you earned, you can request verification and correction.

**Contact:**

### 3.3 NGB-22 (Georgia Army National Guard)

National Guard members receive an NGB Form 22 (not a DD-214) upon separation. Georgia National Guard records are state-managed, not federal.

**How to Request:**

| Method | Details |

## 4. College Credits from Military Service

This section covers every path to converting your military training and experience into college credits.

### 4.1 Joint Services Transcript (JST)

The JST is your military transcript. It translates your training, MOS, and experience into ACE-recommended college credits.

**How to Request:**

| Step | Details |
|------|---------|
| **Website** | [jst.doded.mil](https://jst.doded.mil/) |
| **Registration** | Create account at [jst.doded.mil/jst/registration](https://jst.doded.mil/jst/registration) |
| **Covers** | Army, Marine Corps, Navy, Coast Guard (all your branches) |
| **What It Shows** | Military course completions with credit recommendations, MOS descriptions with skill levels and credit recommendations, college-level test scores |
| **Cost** | FREE |
| **Accepted By** | 2,300+ colleges and universities |

**What You'll Get:**
- Your USMC boot camp training: typically 4-8 lower-division credits (physical education, leadership, first aid)
- MOS school training: varies by MOS, typically 6-15 credits in technical/vocational areas
- DLAB and any language training: potentially 3-12 credits in foreign language or linguistics
- NCO/leadership courses: 3-6 credits in management/leadership
- National Guard training: additional credits for any Army schools/courses completed

**Typical USMC Credit Breakdown:**
- Marine Corps Recruit Training (Boot Camp): 4-6 credits (physical education, first aid, general elective)
- MOS School: 6-15 credits depending on MOS length and complexity
- Corporal's Course / NCO training: 3-6 credits in leadership/management
- Additional military education: varies

### 4.2 ACE Military Guide

The American Council on Education (ACE) evaluates military training and recommends college credit equivalents. You can look up your specific courses.

**How to Use:**
1. Go to [acenet.edu/Programs-Services/Pages/Credit-Transcripts/Military-Guide-Online.aspx](https://www.acenet.edu/Programs-Services/Pages/Credit-Transcripts/Military-Guide-Online.aspx)
2. Search by MOS, course number, or training title
3. See the recommended credit type (upper/lower division) and number of credits
4. Share this with your target school's registrar

## 5. GI Bill Eligibility Analysis

### 5.1 Post-9/11 GI Bill (Chapter 33) -- LIKELY ELIGIBLE

This is the most valuable education benefit. Based on your service:

| Factor | Your Situation |
|--------|---------------|
| **Active Duty After 9/11** | YES -- Mar 2003 to Dec 2003 = approximately 9-10 months |
| **Minimum 90 Days** | YES -- far exceeds 90-day minimum |
| **Honorable Discharge** | YES |

**Benefit Percentage Calculation:**

Your active duty service from Mar 2003 - Dec 2003 = approximately 275 days (roughly 9 months).

| Days of Active Duty | Benefit Level |
|---------------------|---------------|
| 90-179 days | 50% |
| **180-544 days** | **60%** |
| 545-729 days | 70% |

**Your estimated benefit: 60% of full Post-9/11 GI Bill benefits.**

However, ADSW time may also count toward aggregate active duty. If your ADSW orders plus active duty total more than 545 days, you could qualify for 70-80%.

**Post-9/11 GI Bill Benefits at 60% (2025-2026 rates):**
- Tuition & fees: Paid directly to school (up to 60% of the maximum, which varies by state)
- Monthly housing allowance: 60% of the E-5 with dependents BAH rate for your school's zip code
- Books & supplies: Up to $600/year (60% of $1,000)
- Duration: Up to 36 months of benefits

**CRITICAL ACTION:** Contact the VA at 1-888-442-4551 or visit [va.gov/education](https://www.va.gov/education/about-gi-bill-benefits/post-9-11/) to get your official Certificate of Eligibility (COE). The VA will calculate your exact percentage based on all qualifying active duty time.
