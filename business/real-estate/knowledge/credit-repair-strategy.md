# 1. CREDIT BUREAU SYSTEMS DEEP DIVE

## The Big Three: How They Actually Work

### Equifax
- **Headquarters:** Atlanta, GA
- **Data:** 800+ million consumers, 88 million businesses worldwide
- **Reporting cycle:** Furnishers report monthly; data updates within 24-72 hours of receipt
- **Dispute portal:** equifax.com/personal/disputes
- **Phone:** 1-866-349-5191
- **Freeze/unfreeze:** Free under federal law
- **Unique quirk:** Uses its own proprietary scoring models in addition to FICO. Was breached in 2017 (147 million records). Fined $15 million by CFPB in January 2025 for shoddy dispute investigations.

### Experian
- **Headquarters:** Costa Mesa, CA (Irish parent company)
- **Data:** 1.4 billion consumer records globally
- **Reporting cycle:** Furnishers report monthly on statement closing dates
- **Dispute portal:** experian.com/disputes
- **Phone:** 1-888-397-3742
- **Freeze/unfreeze:** Free under federal law
- **Unique quirk:** Owns Experian Boost (utility/rent reporting). CFPB sued Experian in January 2025 for conducting "sham investigations" of consumer disputes rather than properly reviewing them.

### TransUnion
- **Headquarters:** Chicago, IL
- **Data:** 1 billion consumers in 30+ countries
- **Reporting cycle:** Monthly furnisher updates
- **Dispute portal:** transunion.com/credit-disputes
- **Phone:** 1-800-916-8800
- **Freeze/unfreeze:** Free under federal law
- **Unique quirk:** Offers TrueVision scoring model alongside FICO

### How Bureau Data Flows
```
Creditor/Furnisher → Metro 2 Format (426-character record) → e-OSCAR System → Bureau Database → Your Credit File
```

Furnishers (banks, lenders, collectors) send monthly data updates in Metro 2 format. Each account gets coded with:
- Account status (open, closed, collection)
- Payment status (current, 30/60/90/120+ days late)
- Balance, credit limit, payment amount
- Date opened, date reported, date of last activity

**Key insight:** Bureaus do NOT independently verify data. They are data aggregators. They trust furnishers. This is the entire basis for dispute strategies — forcing verification of data that was never verified in the first place.

## FICO Scoring Models: What Actually Matters

### FICO Score Breakdown (All Versions)

| Factor | Weight | What It Measures |
|--------|--------|-----------------|
| **Payment History** | **35%** | On-time payments, late payments (30/60/90/120+ days), collections, bankruptcies |
| **Amounts Owed (Utilization)** | **30%** | Credit card balances vs. limits, loan balances vs. original amounts |
| **Length of Credit History** | **15%** | Age of oldest account, average age of all accounts, age of newest account |
| **Credit Mix** | **10%** | Variety of account types (revolving, installment, mortgage) |
| **New Credit (Inquiries)** | **10%** | Hard inquiries in last 12 months, recently opened accounts |

### FICO 8 (Most Widely Used — THIS IS THE ONE THAT MATTERS NOW)
- **Used by:** Most credit card issuers, auto lenders, personal loan lenders
- **Score range:** 300-850
- **Key behaviors:**
  - Ignores collection accounts with original balance under $100
  - Single late payment hurts more if you have otherwise clean history
  - Authorized user accounts ARE counted (this helps you)
  - Medical and non-medical collections treated the same
  - Paid collections STILL count against you

### FICO 9
- **Used by:** Some lenders (adoption has been slow)
- **Score range:** 300-850
- **Key differences from FICO 8:**
  - Paid collections are IGNORED (score is not affected once paid)
  - Medical collections weighted LESS than other collections
  - Rental payment history CAN be factored in (if reported)
  - Authorized user tradelines given slightly less weight

### FICO 10 and FICO 10T
- **Status:** Released 2020, adoption still slow as of 2026
- **Score range:** 300-850
- **Key differences:**
  - FICO 10T uses TRENDED DATA — looks at 24 months of payment behavior, not just a snapshot
  - Rewards consumers who consistently pay down balances over time
  - Penalizes consumers who carry increasing balances
  - Personal loans used for debt consolidation are tracked — if you consolidate then rack up new debt, score drops harder
  - More predictive than FICO 8/9

### VantageScore 3.0 / 4.0
- **Created by:** The three bureaus jointly (competitor to FICO)
- **Used by:** Credit Karma, some fintech lenders, landlords
- **Score range:** 300-850
- **Key differences:**
  - Can score consumers with as little as 1 month of history
  - Paid collections ignored
  - Medical collections given less weight
  - Trended data in VantageScore 4.0
- **Reality check:** Most mortgage lenders, auto lenders, and credit card issuers use FICO, not VantageScore. Do NOT rely on Credit Karma scores as your real score. They use VantageScore, which can be 20-80 points different from your actual FICO.

### What Moves the Score FASTEST

| Action | Score Impact | Timeline |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| Remove collection account | +25 to +100 points | 30-60 days after removal |
| Pay down credit utilization from 50% to under 9% | +20 to +50 points | 1 billing cycle (30 days) |
| Become authorized user on aged account | +50 to +250 points | 30-60 days |
| Remove late payment from report | +15 to +40 points per late payment | 30-60 days |
| Open secured credit card (thin file) | +10 to +30 points | 3-6 months |
| Hard inquiry drops off (12 months) | +5 to +10 points | Automatic |
| Dispute and remove inaccurate item | Varies widely | 30-45 days |

# 2. DISPUTE STRATEGIES (THE ACTUAL TACTICS)

## The Dispute Hierarchy: What to Use When

```
LEVEL 1: Bureau Dispute (Section 611) — Start here. Fast, forces 30-day investigation.
LEVEL 2: Furnisher Dispute (Section 623) — If bureau dispute fails, go directly to the source.
LEVEL 3: Debt Validation (FDCPA Section 809) — For collection accounts. Forces collector to prove you owe it.
LEVEL 4: Metro 2 Compliance Dispute — Technical disputes based on reporting format errors.
LEVEL 5: CFPB Complaint — Nuclear option. Forces response in 15 days.
LEVEL 6: Attorney General Complaint + FTC — If all else fails.
LEVEL 7: Litigation / FCRA Violation Lawsuit — Real leverage. Statutory damages of $100-$1,000 per violation.
```

---

## Section 609: What Works vs. What Is Myth

### The Truth About 609 Letters
Section 609 of the FCRA gives you the right to REQUEST YOUR FILE DISCLOSURE — meaning you can ask the bureau to show you everything in your file and the sources of that information.

**What Section 609 ACTUALLY says:** You have the right to see all information in your consumer file, the sources of that information, and every entity that received your report in the last 2 years (1 year for employment).

**The myth:** "Send a 609 letter and if the bureau cannot produce a signed contract or original document, they must delete the account." This is FALSE. The FCRA does NOT require bureaus to maintain or produce signed contracts. They only need to show that a furnisher reported the data.

**What DOES work with 609:**
- Requesting full file disclosure to identify ALL accounts and inquiries on your report
- Identifying accounts you do not recognize (possible identity theft or mixed file)
- Getting the names and addresses of every furnisher reporting on your file
- Using the information gathered to fuel Section 611 disputes

**Bottom line:** Use 609 as an intelligence-gathering tool, NOT as a dispute tool. The dispute power comes from Section 611.

## Section 611: Investigation Disputes (YOUR PRIMARY WEAPON)

### How It Works
Under FCRA Section 611, when you dispute information with a credit bureau:

1. Bureau MUST investigate within **30 days** (can extend to 45 days only if you submit additional info during the investigation)
2. Bureau MUST forward your dispute and ALL relevant information to the furnisher within **5 business days**
3. Furnisher MUST investigate and report results back to the bureau
4. If the furnisher **cannot verify** the information, the bureau MUST delete it
5. Bureau MUST notify you in writing of the results within 5 business days of completion
6. If the dispute results in a change, bureau MUST notify ALL bureaus who received the information in the past 6 months

### Dispute Reasons That Force Investigation (Use These Exact Phrases)
- "This account does not belong to me"
- "The balance reported is inaccurate"
- "The payment history is incorrect — I was never 30/60/90 days late on [date]"
- "The date of last activity is incorrect"
- "The account status is incorrectly reported"
- "This account was included in bankruptcy and should show $0 balance"
- "The credit limit / high balance is not being reported accurately"
- "I am not the account holder — I am only an authorized user" (or vice versa)
- "This is a duplicate account"

### Strategic Dispute Rules
1. **Never dispute more than 3-5 items per bureau per round.** Disputing everything at once gets flagged as "frivolous."
2. **Never use template letters from the internet verbatim.** Bureaus have databases of known templates and auto-reject them.
3. **Stagger disputes across bureaus.** Hit Equifax first, then Experian 2 weeks later, then TransUnion 2 weeks after that. This prevents coordinated "frivolous" rejections.
4. **Always send disputes via CERTIFIED MAIL with return receipt.** Never dispute online — online disputes waive certain FCRA rights and limit your ability to sue.
5. **Keep copies of EVERYTHING.** Every letter sent, every response received, every green card from certified mail.
6. **Dispute one issue per item.** If an account has a wrong balance AND a wrong payment history, dispute the balance first. If that fails, dispute the payment history in round 2.

## Metro 2 Compliance Disputes (TECHNICAL POWER MOVE)

### What Is Metro 2?
Metro 2 is the standardized 426-character data format that all furnishers must use when reporting to credit bureaus. It is maintained by the Consumer Data Industry Association (CDIA). Any deviation from Metro 2 standards is a reporting error — and reportable errors must be corrected or deleted.

### Common Metro 2 Errors to Dispute

| Error Type | Metro 2 Code | Dispute Language |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| Wrong account status | Status Code field | "Account status is coded as [X] when it should be [Y] per Metro 2 standards" |
| Missing payment history | Payment Rating field | "Payment history profile is incomplete — missing months [X] through [Y]" |
| Wrong account type | Account Type code | "Account type is incorrectly coded — this is a [revolving/installment] account, not [X]" |
| Missing credit limit | High Credit/Limit field | "Credit limit/high credit is not being reported, causing inaccurate utilization calculation" |
| Wrong date of first delinquency | DOFD field | "Date of first delinquency is reported as [X] when the actual DOFD is [Y]" |
| Incorrect balance | Current Balance field | "Current balance is reported as $[X] when the actual balance is $[Y]" |
| Wrong payment amount | Scheduled Payment field | "Scheduled monthly payment is incorrectly reported" |
| Account ownership wrong | ECOA Code | "ECOA code is [X] (individual) when it should be [Y] (authorized user)" |

### How to Use Metro 2 Disputes
1. Pull your full credit reports from all three bureaus
2. Compare each account's reported data against your own records
3. Look for ANY discrepancy — even minor ones (wrong high balance, missing credit limit, incorrect payment status for any month)
4. Write a dispute letter citing the specific Metro 2 field and error
5. Reference the CDIA Metro 2 Format manual (available at cdiaonline.org)

**Why this works:** Most furnishers do NOT have perfectly clean Metro 2 reporting. The e-OSCAR automated dispute system often cannot reconcile technical Metro 2 challenges, leading to deletions.

## Goodwill Letters for Late Payments

### When to Use
- You have an account with a creditor where you have an OVERALL good payment history
- You had 1-2 late payments due to a specific circumstance (medical, military deployment, emergency)
- The account is currently in good standing

### Success Rate Data
- 79% success rate when borrower has 12+ months of on-time payments
- 40-50% overall success rate (myFICO data)
- 60% success rate for first-time requests on one-time errors
- Potential score boost: 40-50 points per removed late payment

### Goodwill Letter Structure
1. Open with appreciation for the relationship
2. Reference your long history of on-time payments
3. Briefly explain the circumstance (keep it to 2-3 sentences)
4. Do NOT make excuses — take responsibility
5. Request the removal as a "goodwill gesture"
6. Close with commitment to continued timely payment
7. Keep the entire letter under 300 words
8. Include account number and specific late payment date(s)

### Pro Tips
- Call the creditor first and ask to speak to a supervisor in the "customer retention" or "executive office" department
- If phone call fails, send a written letter to the executive office address (not the general PO box)
- If first attempt fails, try again in 3 months with a different approach
- Attach proof: bank statements showing the payment, medical bills, deployment orders, etc.
- Multiple attempts over 3-6 months can succeed even if the first one fails

## Pay-for-Delete Negotiation

### What It Is
An agreement where you pay a collection account (full balance or settled amount) in exchange for the collector removing the account from your credit report entirely.

### Critical Rules
1. **NEVER pay a collection account before getting a pay-for-delete agreement IN WRITING**
2. Under FICO 8 (most widely used model), paid collections STILL count against you — so paying without deletion gains you NOTHING for your FICO 8 score
3. Under FICO 9 and VantageScore 3.0+, paid collections are ignored — so if a lender uses these models, you benefit from paying even without deletion
4. Pay-for-delete is NOT legally binding even if agreed to in writing — but most collectors will honor it because they want the money
5. Always negotiate the amount DOWN before requesting deletion

### Negotiation Script (Phone)
```
"Hi, I'm calling about account number [X]. I'd like to resolve this account today.
I can offer [50% of balance] as a lump sum payment, and in exchange, I need
the account completely removed from all three credit bureau reports — Equifax,
Experian, and TransUnion. Can you authorize that?"

[If they say no to deletion:]
"I understand. However, I'm only willing to make this payment if the account
is removed. Under FICO 8, a paid collection still hurts my score the same as
an unpaid one, so there's no incentive for me to pay unless it's removed.
Can you escalate this to a supervisor who can authorize deletion?"

[If they agree:]
"Thank you. Before I provide payment, I need this agreement in writing —
either emailed or mailed to me — confirming that upon receipt of payment,
the account will be deleted from all three credit bureaus within 30 days.
Can you send that to me today?"
```

### Written Pay-for-Delete Steps
1. Send a written offer via certified mail
2. Offer 25-50% of the balance as a starting point
3. State that payment is contingent on written agreement to delete
4. Give them 15 days to respond
5. If they agree, get it in writing before sending any payment
6. Pay via cashier's check or money order (NEVER give bank account or card info)
7. After payment, follow up in 30 days; if not deleted, send a copy of the agreement with a demand letter

## Debt Validation Under FDCPA (Section 809)

### Your Right
Within 30 days of a debt collector's FIRST contact with you, you can demand they validate the debt. Under FDCPA Section 809:
- Collector MUST send you a written validation notice within 5 days of first contact
- You have 30 days from receiving that notice to dispute and request validation
- Once you request validation, collector MUST STOP all collection activity until they provide:
  - The amount of the debt
  - The name of the original creditor
  - Proof that they are authorized to collect
  - Proof that the debt is yours

### Strategic Use
1. Send debt validation letters to EVERY collection account on your reports
2. Send via certified mail, return receipt requested
3. If the collector cannot validate within 30 days, they must STOP collection AND the account should be removed from your credit reports
4. Many collectors (especially junk debt buyers who purchased old debt for pennies) cannot produce proper validation
5. If they continue collecting without validating, they are in VIOLATION of the FDCPA — you can sue for $1,000 per violation plus attorney fees

### Timing Strategy
- Send debt validation requests FIRST (before disputing with bureaus)
- Wait 35 days for a response
- If no response: dispute with bureaus citing "collector failed to validate debt per FDCPA Section 809"
- If they respond with insufficient validation: dispute with bureaus citing "debt not properly validated — documentation provided is insufficient"

## Cease and Desist Strategies

### When to Use
- You have a debt that is past the statute of limitations (SOL) — in Georgia, the SOL is 6 years for most debts
- A collector is harassing you (calling multiple times per day, calling before 8 AM or after 9 PM, contacting third parties)
- You have no intention of paying the debt and want all communication to stop

### What Cease and Desist Does
Under FDCPA, once a collector receives your written cease and desist letter, they can ONLY contact you to:
1. Acknowledge receipt of the cease and desist
2. Notify you of a specific action they intend to take (like filing a lawsuit)
3. They CANNOT call you, send letters demanding payment, or contact anyone else about the debt

### Warning
- Cease and desist does NOT make the debt go away
- The collector can still sue you (and the cease and desist sometimes prompts them to)
- The account can still remain on your credit report
- Use this strategically — it is best for time-barred debts where the collector has no legal basis to sue

## CFPB Complaints as Leverage

### How It Works
The Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (consumerfinance.gov/complaint) accepts complaints against credit bureaus, lenders, and debt collectors. When you file:
1. CFPB forwards your complaint to the company
2. Company MUST respond within **15 days** (vs. 30 days for normal disputes)
3. Company response is tracked and published in the CFPB complaint database
4. Pattern of complaints can trigger CFPB enforcement action

### 2025-2026 Updates
- Credit reporting accounts for 88% of all CFPB complaints
- CFPB complaint volume jumped from 542,300 in 2020 to 5.6 million in 2025
- **NEW REQUIREMENT:** CFPB now requires you to dispute directly with the credit bureau FIRST before filing a CFPB complaint. If the bureau tells CFPB you did not dispute directly first, CFPB will discontinue processing your complaint.

### Strategy
1. Always dispute directly with the bureau FIRST (via certified mail)
2. Wait 30-35 days for response
3. If response is unsatisfactory or you get no response, THEN file a CFPB complaint
4. In the CFPB complaint, reference your original dispute, the date sent, and the certified mail tracking number
5. Attach copies of your original dispute letter and the bureau's response (or lack thereof)
6. CFPB complaints often result in items being deleted that normal disputes failed to remove

# 3. IRS TAX LIEN / COLLECTION IMPACT

## The Good News: Tax Liens Are OFF Credit Reports

### What Happened in 2018
In April 2018, all three major credit bureaus (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion) stopped including tax liens on consumer credit reports as part of the National Consumer Assistance Plan (NCAP). This means:

- **Your $140K IRS debt does NOT appear on your Equifax, Experian, or TransUnion credit reports**
- **It does NOT directly impact your FICO score**
- Any previously reported tax liens were removed and scores likely increased

### What This Means for Jamaal
Your IRS debt of ~$140K is NOT dragging your credit score down through the credit bureaus. The 500 score is caused by OTHER factors — likely collections, late payments, high utilization, or thin credit file. This is actually good news because it means the score can be rebuilt without resolving the IRS situation first.

## The Bad News: It Still Shows Elsewhere

### Where the IRS Debt DOES Show
1. **LexisNexis:** Federal tax liens are still public records and show in LexisNexis reports
2. **Public records search:** Anyone doing a thorough background check will find it
3. **Mortgage lending:** FHA/VA/conventional mortgage underwriters check for federal tax liens beyond credit reports. A Notice of Federal Tax Lien (NFTL) will show up in a title search and can block real estate closings.
4. **IRS can file a Notice of Federal Tax Lien (NFTL):** For debts over $10,000, the IRS can file an NFTL which attaches to your property. While this does not appear on credit reports anymore, it DOES attach to any real property you own.

### How to Ensure IRS Collections Do Not Re-Report
1. Tax liens cannot re-appear on credit reports — the bureaus no longer accept them
2. However, if the IRS assigns your debt to a Private Collection Agency (PCA), that PCA could attempt to report a collection account. This is different from a tax lien.
3. **Monitor your reports** monthly. If ANY IRS-related collection appears, dispute immediately:
   - "This is a federal tax obligation. Under the NCAP agreement effective April 2018, tax liens and tax-related collections are excluded from consumer credit reports."
4. The IRS currently uses three PCAs: CBE Group, Coast Professional, and ConServe. If any of them contact you, send a debt validation letter AND a cease and desist if you are pursuing CNC status.

## Currently Not Collectible (CNC) Status

### What It Is
CNC status tells the IRS to temporarily stop collection efforts because you cannot afford to pay your basic living expenses AND your tax debt.

### Why Jamaal Qualifies
- Monthly income: $3,402 (VA disability + SS rep payee)
- IRS uses allowable expense standards that in 2025 were adjusted upward ~5% for inflation
- If your monthly disposable income is $25 or less after allowable expenses, you qualify
- VA disability income and SS rep payee income, combined with housing, food, transportation, and healthcare costs in Atlanta, GA, very likely leaves less than $25/month disposable

### What CNC Does
- IRS stops wage garnishment (not applicable — VA disability cannot be garnished anyway)
- IRS stops bank levies
- IRS stops collection notices
- IRS keeps your tax refunds and applies them to the debt
- Penalties and interest CONTINUE to accrue (7% interest per year in 2025, 0.5%/month late payment penalty up to 25%)
- IRS reviews your status annually or when you file new returns

### What CNC Does NOT Do
- Does not eliminate the debt
- Does not stop the 10-year collection statute of limitations from running (this is actually GOOD — after 10 years from assessment, the debt expires)
- Does not prevent the IRS from filing a Notice of Federal Tax Lien (NFTL) for debts over $10,000
- Does not affect your credit score (IRS does not report to bureaus)

### How to Get CNC Status
1. Call the IRS: 1-800-829-1040
2. Explain financial hardship and request CNC / hardship status
3. You will need to provide financial information:
   - Form 433-F (Collection Information Statement) or Form 433-A (detailed)
   - 3 months of bank statements
   - Pay stubs / income documentation
   - Monthly expense documentation
4. IRS compares your income to their Allowable Living Expense (ALE) standards
5. If income minus ALE = $25 or less, CNC is granted

### The 10-Year Clock (CSED)
The IRS has a Collection Statute Expiration Date (CSED) — generally 10 years from the date of assessment. After the CSED, the debt is legally uncollectible and is written off.

**Critical:** Find out the CSED for each tax year you owe. If the oldest debts are approaching 10 years, CNC status lets the clock run out. Request your IRS account transcripts (Form 4506-T) to get exact assessment dates.

### Action Steps for IRS Situation
1. Request IRS account transcripts for all years with balances (call 1-800-908-9946 or use IRS.gov online)
2. Calculate the CSED for each tax year
3. Apply for CNC status using Form 433-F
4. If the IRS has already filed an NFTL, request a lien withdrawal (Form 12277) once CNC is granted — argue that withdrawal is in the best interest of both the government and taxpayer
5. Monitor credit reports monthly for any IRS-related reporting
6. NEVER agree to an installment agreement or Offer in Compromise without calculating whether CNC + CSED expiration is a better strategy

# 4. RAPID SCORE BUILDING TACTICS

## Authorized User Tradelines (FASTEST SCORE BOOST)

### Why This Is Priority #1
Tia has good credit. Adding Jamaal as an authorized user on her oldest, highest-limit, lowest-utilization credit card(s) can boost his score by 50-250 points within 30-60 days.

### How It Works
- Tia calls her credit card company and adds Jamaal as an authorized user
- The card's ENTIRE history (including years of on-time payments) gets added to Jamaal's credit report
- The card's credit limit gets added to Jamaal's available credit, improving utilization
- Jamaal does NOT need to receive the physical card, use the card, or know the account number
- The account appears on Jamaal's report within 1-2 billing cycles (30-60 days)

### Ideal Tradeline Characteristics
- **Age:** 10+ years (the older the better — this boosts "length of credit history," which is 15% of FICO)
- **Credit limit:** $10,000+ (bigger limit = lower utilization ratio across all accounts)
- **Payment history:** 100% on-time payments (this is what moves the 35% payment history factor)
- **Utilization:** Under 9% (low balance relative to limit)
- **Account type:** Major credit card (Visa, Mastercard, Amex, Discover)

### What to Ask Tia
1. "What is your oldest credit card, what is the limit, and what is your current balance on it?"
2. If the card is 5+ years old, has a $5K+ limit, and utilization is under 30%, add Jamaal as AU
3. If she has multiple qualifying cards, add Jamaal to 2-3 of them for maximum impact

## Optimal Credit Utilization Strategy

### The Utilization Tiers (FICO Impact)

| Utilization Range | FICO Impact | Strategy |
|-------------------|------------|----------|
| 0% | Slightly negative | Scoring models need SOME activity |
| **1-9%** | **Maximum positive impact** | **THE TARGET** |
| 10-29% | Good, minimal penalty | Acceptable range |
| 30-49% | Moderate penalty | Old "30% rule" is outdated |
| 50-74% | Significant penalty | Actively hurting your score |
| 75%+ | Severe penalty | Every point of utilization here costs you |

### Statement Date vs. Due Date: The Key Distinction

**Statement closing date:** The date each month when the credit card company takes a "snapshot" of your balance and generates your statement. THIS is the balance that gets reported to the credit bureaus.

**Due date:** The date by which you must make at least the minimum payment to avoid a late payment. This is typically 21-25 days AFTER the statement closing date.

### The Optimization Play
1. Find out the statement closing date for each of your credit cards (call the issuer or check your online account)
2. Pay down your balance to 1-9% of the credit limit BEFORE the statement closing date
3. The low balance gets reported to bureaus
4. Then pay the remaining statement balance by the due date
5. Result: 100% on-time payment history + ultra-low utilization = maximum FICO impact

**Example:**
- Card: $500 credit limit
- Statement closing date: 15th of each month
- Target balance on the 15th: $5-$45 (1-9% of $500)
- Pay on the 12th or 13th to get the balance down
- Statement generates showing $5-$45 balance — this is what bureaus see
- Pay the $5-$45 statement balance by the due date (around the 8th of the next month)

### Per-Card vs. Overall Utilization
FICO calculates BOTH:
1. **Individual card utilization** (balance vs. limit on each card)
2. **Aggregate utilization** (total balances across all cards vs. total limits across all cards)

Both matter. Keep EACH card under 9% AND keep the total under 9%.
