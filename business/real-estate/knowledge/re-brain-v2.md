## WHOLESALING ‚Äî EXPERT KNOWLEDGE BASE

### What Wholesaling IS
- You find distressed/discounted properties and get them under contract
- You assign that contract (your equitable interest) to a cash buyer for an assignment fee
- You NEVER own the property, NEVER do repairs, NEVER need a loan
- You are an information broker and deal finder ‚Äî a logistics business
- Legal in all 50 states but regulations are tightening (see compliance section)

### The Wholesaling Process (Step by Step)
1. **Lead Generation** ‚Äî Find motivated sellers via direct mail, cold calling, driving for dollars, PPC, bandit signs, skip tracing, MLS expired/withdrawn, probate lists, pre-foreclosure lists, tax delinquent lists, absentee owners, code violations, inherited properties
2. **Lead Qualification** ‚Äî Determine motivation level (1-10), timeline, property condition, financial situation, asking price vs. realistic value
3. **Appointment / Property Visit** ‚Äî Walk the property, estimate repairs, build rapport with seller
4. **Run the Numbers** ‚Äî ARV, repair costs, MAO using 70% rule, rental income potential
5. **Make the Offer** ‚Äî Present purchase agreement with assignment clause, earnest money ($500-$2,000)
6. **Get Under Contract** ‚Äî Sign PSA with inspection period (10-14 days)
7. **Disposition (Find Your Buyer)** ‚Äî Blast deal to cash buyer list, post on investor forums, call top buyers
8. **Assign the Contract** ‚Äî Sign Assignment of Contract with buyer, collect non-refundable deposit
9. **Close at Title Company** ‚Äî Title company handles closing, pays seller, cuts you assignment fee check
10. **Reinvest** ‚Äî Put profits back into marketing. Never stop lead generating.

### Key Formulas
```
MAO (Maximum Allowable Offer) = ARV x 0.70 - Repair Costs - Your Assignment Fee
Assignment Fee = Buyer's Purchase Price - Your Contract Price
Wholesale Spread = ARV - Repair Costs - Buyer's Desired Profit - Your Fee
```

## SUBJECT-TO DEALS ‚Äî EXPERT KNOWLEDGE BASE

### What Subject-To IS
- You purchase a property "subject to" the existing mortgage staying in place
- Seller transfers the deed to you ‚Äî you OWN the property
- Seller's loan stays in seller's name, but YOU make the payments
- You control, rent, build equity ‚Äî without qualifying for a new loan
- Seller gets relief from payments, avoids foreclosure, preserves credit

### Why Sellers Say Yes
- Facing foreclosure, underwater, divorce, job relocation, inherited property, tired landlord, behind on payments, can't sell traditionally due to condition or negative equity

### Subject-To Deal Structure
1. Find motivated seller (filter for: HAS a mortgage, wants OUT)
2. Verify loan details: lender, servicer, balance, rate, payment (PITI), missed payments, loan type, prepayment penalty, escrow balance
3. Negotiate: what does seller need at closing? ($0? Back payments? Small cash?)
4. Execute paperwork (with attorney): Sub-To Purchase Agreement, Warranty Deed, Authorization to Release Info, Limited POA, CYA Letter / Seller Disclosure
5. Close: record deed, set up autopay to servicer, change/add insurance, set up PM
6. Exit: hold as rental, refinance, sell on wrap, lease option, sell outright

### The Due-On-Sale Clause
**What it is:** Contractual right (NOT a law) allowing lender to demand full repayment if property transfers without consent. Lender MAY call, doesn't HAVE to.

**Reality:** Enforcement is extremely rare when payments are current. Lenders lose money on foreclosure. In decades of sub-to investing, actual calls are nearly unheard of.

**Garn-St. Germain Act Exceptions (lender CANNOT call due):**
- Transfer to spouse/children
- Transfer due to death
- Transfer to living trust where borrower is beneficiary AND occupant
- Divorce/separation transfers

**Land Trust Strategy:**
- Seller transfers property into revocable land trust (seller initially beneficiary)
- Seller assigns beneficial interest to you/your LLC
- Lender sees trust (assumes estate planning), doesn't see sale
- NOT bulletproof for investment properties (occupancy requirement), but enforcement remains extremely rare
- Always consult YOUR attorney

**Risk Mitigation:**
- ALWAYS keep payments current
- Set up autopay to servicer
- Maintain insurance (lapse = lender alert)
- Don't contact lender unnecessarily
- Use land trust for privacy
- Have refinance exit plan
- Keep $5K-$10K reserves per sub-to property

### Subject-To Math Example
```
Existing Loan:    $180,000 @ 3.5% (locked 2021)
Monthly PITI:     $1,100
Property FMV:     $230,000
Monthly Rent:     $1,800
Cash to Seller:   $3,000 (back payments + moving money)

Cash Flow:        $1,800 - $1,100 - $150 (PM) - $100 (reserves) = $450/mo
Cash-on-Cash:     ($450 x 12) / $3,000 = 180%
Instant Equity:   $230,000 - $180,000 = $50,000
```

### Wraparound Mortgage (Advanced)
- Buy sub-to existing mortgage, sell to new buyer with seller financing at HIGHER rate
- Collect buyer's payment, pay existing loan, keep the spread
- Example: Existing 3.5%, wrap at 7% ‚Äî pocket rate spread + principal difference
- MUST use attorney and loan servicing company
