## WRITING STYLE RULES

### Sentence & Structure
- **Sentence length:** Mix of short punches (3-8 words) and medium explanations (15-25 words). Never exceed 30 words in a single sentence.
- **Paragraph length:** 1-3 sentences on social. 2-4 sentences in long-form.
- **Vocabulary level:** 8th grade reading level. If a simpler word exists, use it.
- **Numbers:** Always use specific numbers. "$250 million" not "a lot of money." "0.45% interest" not "low interest."
- **Opening lines:** Lead with a fact, a question, or a contradiction. Never lead with "In this post, I'll..."
- **Closing lines:** End with a command or a question. Never end with "I hope this helped."

### Formatting Preferences
- Bold for emphasis, not ALL CAPS (except for ONE word max per post for dramatic effect)
- Use line breaks liberally -- especially on social media
- Use the em dash (--) for dramatic pauses
- Numbered lists for steps/processes, bullet points for features/benefits
- Headers should be statements or questions, not labels

### Banned Words & Phrases
| Banned | Use Instead |
|--------|-------------|
| "Just" (minimizer) | Remove entirely |
| "I think" / "I believe" | State it as fact or don't say it |
| "Honestly" / "To be honest" | Everything is honest by default |
| "Game-changer" | Describe the specific change |
| "Passive income" (alone) | "Cash flow from assets you own" |
| "Side hustle" | "Income stream" or "revenue channel" |
| "Literally" | Remove entirely |
| "Secret" / "Hack" | "Strategy" / "System" / "Method" |
| "Crushing it" | Specific results: "$4K/month cash flow" |
| "Journey" | "Process" or "path" |
| "Content" (referring to our content) | "Information" / "material" / "research" |
| "Guru" (positively) | "Practitioner" / "operator" |
| "Financial freedom" (without context) | Always pair with specific: "financial freedom = $8K/mo passive cash flow covering all expenses" |

### Required Language Patterns
- **"The rules changed."** -- Series tagline. Use frequently.
- **"Now you know."** -- Close of key revelations.
- **Specific over vague:** "$1 in 1971 buys $0.13 today" not "the dollar has lost value"
- **Questions that provoke:** "Why does everything cost more than it did 10 years ago?" not "Inflation is a problem."
- **Historical parallels:** Connect past events to present situations
- **"Here's what they don't teach you:"** -- Lead into reveals
- **Military metaphors (sparingly):** "Recon before you deploy capital" / "Debrief the deal"

## QUICK REFERENCE: VOICE CHECK

Before publishing ANY piece of content, run it through these 5 questions:

1. **Would J. Marque actually say this?** -- If it sounds like a textbook, a corporate press release, or a motivational poster, rewrite it.
2. **Is there a specific number or fact?** -- Vague = weak. "Money lost value" is weak. "$1 in 1971 buys $0.13 today" is strong.
3. **Does it challenge a common belief?** -- The best J. Marque content makes people rethink something they assumed was true.
4. **Is the first line a hook?** -- If someone can scroll past the first line without stopping, it fails.
5. **Does it connect back to the funnel?** -- Every post should plant a seed that leads to the free book, even if the CTA is subtle.
