He had $340K across two retirement accounts. One 401(k) from his current employer. One IRA from a previous job. Both in target-date funds. Both earning 6–8% in good years, losing 10–15% in bad ones. Both charging fees he’d never bothered to calculate.

He read Money Decoded. Then he ran the Wealth Drain Calculator from The Wealth Shift.

What he found: his two accounts were losing a combined $7,400/year to management fees and inflation drag. In 20 years, at that rate, he’d lose $148,000 to the system. Not including the opportunity cost of what that money could have produced elsewhere.

What David did:

He repositioned $80K from his old 401(k) into a self-directed IRA. No penalty. No taxes (it was a direct rollover). The process took about 3 weeks using the 21-step checklist.

Then he used that SDIRA to fund his first BRRRR deal:
